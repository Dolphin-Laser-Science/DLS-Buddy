Forward model (the program's own physics, run forwards):
  Gamma_VV = q^2 D_t           (polarized field decay rate)
  Gamma_VH = q^2 D_t + 6 D_r   (depolarized: + rotational term)
  g2(tau)-1 = beta exp(-2 Gamma tau)   (homodyne, Siegert)
  => D_r = (Gamma_VH - Gamma_VV)/6;  multi-angle Gamma_VH vs q^2:
     slope = D_t, intercept = 6 D_r.

RECOMMENDED RECOVERY (esp. for the noisy VH set): take D_t from the
VV channel (Gamma_VV = q^2 D_t is the whole VV signal, cleanly
measured), and D_r from Gamma_VH - Gamma_VV. Fitting D_t as the SLOPE
of Gamma_VH vs q^2 is ill-conditioned when rotation dominates (the
slope is tiny next to the 6 D_r intercept), so it degrades fast under
VH noise; the intercept (6 D_r) stays robust.
  Refs: Zero & Pecora 1982 (Eqs. III.1/III.2); Pecora 1964;
        rod D_t,D_r from Tirado & Garcia de la Torre 1984.
